Planets

Venus

Nickname: The Evening Star

Surface Temperature: ~475°C (hottest planet in the Solar System)

Atmosphere: Thick with carbon dioxide and clouds of sulfuric acid, causing a runaway greenhouse effect.

Fun Fact: Venus rotates in the opposite direction to most planets, and a day on Venus is longer than a year!

Mars

Nickname: The Red Planet

Surface: Rocky with iron oxide (rust), giving it its red color.

Exploration: Home to rovers like Perseverance and Curiosity.

Fun Fact: Mars has the tallest volcano in the Solar System, Olympus Mons, which is nearly three times the height of Mount Everest!

Jupiter

Nickname: The Gas Giant

Size: Largest planet in the Solar System (1,300 Earths could fit inside it).

Great Red Spot: A massive storm larger than Earth, raging for centuries.

Fun Fact: Jupiter has 95 confirmed moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the Solar System.

Saturn

Nickname: The Ringed Planet

Rings: Made of ice and rock, stretching over 175,000 miles wide but only about 30 feet thick.

Atmosphere: Composed mostly of hydrogen and helium.

Fun Fact: Saturn’s moon Titan has rivers and lakes of liquid methane, making it one of the most Earth-like bodies in the Solar System.
